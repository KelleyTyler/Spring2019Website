Tutorial For Animation In Wick

This is where I go into a Tutorial for Animation: I debated with myself as to the exact system or program I should make my tutorial for, and there are several options with regards to animation software, both free and paid. While I had been originally intending this tutorial to use a program called "Krita", I have recently found something even easier/handier for learning the basics, the web-based editor "Wick".

Step 1:

either opening a new tab or a new window go to WickEditor.com/WickEditor

Before Continuing Make sure you've got the right colors selected; furthermore go into "File", click "Project Settings", and then looking at the "project settings options" on the right-hand side of the screen; make sure your Framerate(designated by the film-strip icon) is set at 15-20


Step 2

draw a circle by selecting the "Ellipse" tool in the left-side toolbar, draw it to the top-left of the white-box, you can drag the circle by hitting the "c" key on your keyboard as well


Step3

notice how there's a light-grey/white frame with a black circle/dot on your timeline, that's a "frame" with something in it", right-click this frame on the timeline, and select "Add Tween Keyframe" (should be the first option).

next drag the edge (should highlight and change your cursor to a double-sided arrow) of this frame (now purple/blue in color) to the "20" frame-mark


Step4

In the middle of your purple bar click on frame 10; then Pressing the 'c' key on your keyboard, and drag the circle towards the bottom middle of the white-area

After this select the "20" frame and drag the circle to the top right-hand side of the white box;

Pressing the "play" button at the bottom left of the timeline-bar, or clicking the "run" button in the menu-bar should provide you with something along the lines of the animated gif below


Step4+

The Next Step is to essentially mess around with scaling, shrinking, and rotation, just as tweens will create/smooth the transitions between "translation", tweens will also create/smooth transitions between rotation and scaling.
