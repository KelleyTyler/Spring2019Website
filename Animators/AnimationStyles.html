ANIMATION STYLES

of Note

Though by no means as comprehensive overview, here's a few types or styles of animation that I know of.

2d Cell/Hand-Drawn/Traditional Animation

Before computers got advanced enough to be capable of rendering meaningful graphics, most animation was done by this very labor and time intensive method of Traditional Hand-Drawn, Cell-Based Animation which it's name from the transparent plastic sheets called Cells on which individual frames of animation were drawn.

Stop Motion

An Animation Style that often relies on physical 3d objects being moved in between individual shots with a camera, stop-motion animation goes back to the dawn of motion pictures

examples of this include many of the early animations by Aardman studio (Wallace and Grommit, Chicken Run, etc.); as well as american works like Gumby and even the first few seasons of South-Park (which used actual pieces of construction-paper rather than drawn illustrations)

2d Computer-Animation

Though 2d Animation Using Computers had existed for quite some time, and was certainly used in enhancing/easing up some of the pipeline for theatrical and television animation, I think 2d CGI Animation really got started in the later 1990's or early 2000's.

I say this in part because, from my recollection; that's when Shockwave and later Flash were created and first marketed.

Though supposedly obsolete by today's standards, Shockwave/Flash played a significant role in pioneering and highlighting some of the basic advantages of 2d animation, notably the ease that comes when using vectors over bitmaps.

The Use of Vectors, and the ease that they present can be explained as such: if at frame-1 a circle is at coordinates (0,0), and at frame-5, the circle is at coordinates(10,10),(both frame 1 and frame 5 representing what are called keyframes) you don't necesarily need to have people drawing frames 2-4.

likewise consider that this goes for rotation, and furthermore realize the savings from not requiring the amount of resources (pencils, ink/brushes, clear cell sheets, xenography machines, etc)

3d CGI

Computer Generated 3d animation, at least as far as the entertainment industry is concerned, first saw widespread use as a means by which to augment or enhance more traditional methods of animation, with films such as Walt Disney's "The Great Mouse Detective"(1986) using 3d animation in the clocktower chase sequence, or live action films such as Jurrassic Park or Tron using CGI to help enhance and complement a combination of stop motion figures, live actors, and animatronics.
